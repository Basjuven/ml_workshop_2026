# Introduction

We live in an era in which large amounts of data are continuously generated by scientific experiments, sensors, mobile devices, and digital platforms. Understanding these data and extracting meaningful information from them has become a fundamental skill in both modern science and education.

This workshop provides a practical introduction to *Machine Learning* as a tool for exploring, analyzing, and modeling real data. Throughout the activities, participants will work with datasets from different scientific areas, such as particle physics, astronomy, geosciences, and health, with the goal of developing intuition about how algorithms can identify patterns, make predictions, and support the construction of knowledge.

The workshop has a dual focus:

- **Student perspective:** learning Python programming, data analysis, and machine learning through guided activities.
- **Teacher perspective:** designing educational experiences that integrate these tools into the classroom.

Rather than learning to use algorithms as black boxes, this workshop seeks to help participants understand what the results mean, how models are built from data, and what their limitations are. In this way, *Machine Learning* is presented as an extension of the scientific method: observe, identify patterns, build models, and validate conclusions.

By the end of the workshop, participants will have developed materials and activities that they can implement with their students to promote data-based learning, programming, and scientific inquiry in real educational contexts.

---

## Workshop Objectives

- Understand what *Machine Learning* is and why it is important in modern science.
- Apply Python programming to analyze data.
- Use machine learning models to identify patterns.
- Interpret results and evaluate their scientific validity.
- Design educational activities based on real data.
